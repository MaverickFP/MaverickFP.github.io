Eclisse Table Lamp

Vintage design chair designed by Vico Magistretti in 1960, the Carimate 892, released by Cassina. The chair is designed for Carimate Golf Club.

Characteristics

Eclisse table lamp designed in 1965 by Vico Magistretti for Artemide won the Compasso D'Oro in 1967 and has become a classic of Italian design. It is an object with simple shapes inspired by the miners' lanterns or thieves, as pointed out the same Magistretti. In '63 while he was on the subway in Milan had an idea to the lantern thinking of Jean Valjean, the protagonist of Les Miserables by Victor Hugo. He made a sketch on the back of the ticket and called his assistant in the studio describing how to combine a couple of balls. The lamp is made from 3 "semifere". The base, the outer shell and inner shell fixed-mobile with manual rotation allows adjustment of the luminous flux recalling, in fact, an eclipse of the moon. Choosing a simple technology, not exaggerated, aesthetic forms based on primary. The dome rotates on its axis can graduate the light output. Lamp: 25W E14

Model - Details

The base area of the lamp was made using three circumferences that were combined with the function CUBIC_HERMITE. A similar method was used for the upper part of the lamp. The inner surface has been realized using the function "Sfera"perfecting the whole with CUBIC_HERMITE.
